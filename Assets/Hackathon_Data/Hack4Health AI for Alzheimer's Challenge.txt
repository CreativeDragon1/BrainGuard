The Challenge: AI for Alzheimer's Detection
Brief Overview: Your challenge is to develop a machine learning model that accurately classifies Alzheimer's Disease from MRI scans. Alzheimer's is a neurodegenerative disease that is notoriously difficult to diagnose early. Recent advances in AI, particularly in deep learning, have shown promise in identifying subtle patterns in brain scans that can indicate the presence of the disease, even in its early stages.
The Dataset
For this competition, we will be using the "Alzheimer MRI Disease Classification Dataset". This dataset contains a series of MRI scans categorized into different stages of Alzheimer's Disease.
Dataset Path: /kaggle/input/alzheimer-mri-disease-classification-dataset

2. Free GPU Access:
Training deep learning models on image data is computationally intensive. We highly recommend using free GPU resources to accelerate your model training.
Google Colab: Provides free access to GPUs. To enable it, open your notebook, go to Runtime -> Change runtime type, and select GPU from the "Hardware accelerator" dropdown.
Kaggle: Notebooks on Kaggle also offer free GPU access. You can enable it in the "Settings" tab of your notebook editor.
3. Ensuring Reproducibility:
Reproducibility is a cornerstone of good science. To ensure your results are consistent, it's crucial to set random seeds at the beginning of your code. This ensures that operations with a random element, like data shuffling or model weight initialization, produce the same result every time.

Suggested Timeline (4 Weeks)
To help you manage your time effectively, here is a suggested timeline.
Week 1: Exploration and Baseline Model
Goal: Understand the data and establish a simple, working model.
Tasks:
Load and preprocess the data using the starter notebook.
Perform Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).
Build and train a simple baseline model (e.g., a basic CNN).
Establish a validation framework to measure performance reliably.
Week 2: Model Development and Iteration
Goal: Improve upon your baseline model.
Tasks:
Experiment with different model architectures (e.g., ResNet, VGG).
Implement transfer learning using pre-trained weights.
Begin hyperparameter tuning and data augmentation.
Week 3: Refinement and Explainability
Goal: Finalize your model and interpret its predictions.
Tasks:
Fine-tune your best-performing model.
Incorporate Explainable AI (XAI) techniques like Grad-CAM.
Analyze model errors and start drafting your presentation.
Week 4: Finalization and Presentation Prep
Goal: Prepare your final submission package.
Tasks:
Clean and comment your code thoroughly.
Finalize your trained model and presentation.
Practice your pitch
Evaluation Criteria

Explainability and Interpretability: Insight into your model's decisions.
Code Quality and Reproducibility: Clean, well-documented, and runnable code.
Presentation and Storytelling: Ability to communicate your project's story and impact.

Code: A link to your final, runnable Google Colab or Kaggle notebook.
